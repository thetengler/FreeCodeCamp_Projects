Witold Pilecki

The Auschwitz Volunteer

"I've been trying to live my life so that in the hour of my death I would rather feel joy, than fear." --Witold Pilecki

Witold Pilecki in his military uniform.

Here's a time line of Witold Pilecki's life:

  • 1901 - Pilecki is born in the town of Olonets, Karelia, in the Russian Empire.
  • 1915 - He and his family flee German occupied Wilno (now: Vilnius, Lithuania) to Mogilev, Byelorussia.
  • 1916 - Pilecki moves to the Russian City of Oryol, where he attends gymnasium and founds a local chapter of the ZHP Scouts Organization.
  • 1920 - Joins the Uhlan Regiment and fights in the crucial Battle of Warsaw and in the Rudniki Forest.
  • 1931 - Marries Maria Pilecka nee Ostrowska, a local school teacher, and they have two children.
  • 1932 - Establishes a cavalry training school in Lida and shortly afterward is appointed commander of the newly-established 1st Lidsky Squadron.
  • 1939 - As a calvary platoon commander, Pilecki takes part in the fight against the German army during the invasion of Poland. After the Polish government's surrender Pilecki went into hiding in Warsaw with his commander, Major Wlodarkiewicz. On November 9th, they founded the Secret Polish Army (Tajna Armia Polska, TAP), one of the first underground organizations in Poland.
  • 1940 - Pilecki presents a plan to his superiors to enter Germany's Auschwitz concentration camp. On September 19th, he deliberatly goes out during a Warsaw street roundup, where he is caught by the Germans and soon sent to Auschwitz. While there he organizes the underground Union of Military Organizations (ZOW).
  • 1943 - Pilecki along with two of his comrades, escapes from Auschwitz, taking with them documents stolen from the Germans.
  • 1943 - Pilecki is promoted to Rotmistrz and joins a secret anti-communist organization formed as a clandestine unit within the Home Army.
  • 1947 - He begins independently collecting evidence of Soviet atrocities committed in Poland during the 1939-1941 occupation as well as evidence of the unlawful arrest and prosecution of Home Army veterans and ex-members of the Polish Armed Forces in the West. On May 8th he is arrested by agents of the Ministry of Public Security.
  • 1948 - Pilecki is executed at the Mokotow Prison in Warsaw.
